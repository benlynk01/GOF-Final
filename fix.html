Technological responses to terrorism

Technological advancements after the attacks

Following these events security was enhanced, fewer passengers flew at once, longer wait times were in security lines. Just to make sure that people weren't there to cause harm.

Later in 2001, The Aviation and Transportation Security Act created the TSA. They started checking baggage that were screened by X-Rays.

In December of the same year, a terrorist named Richard Reid attempted to set off a bomb that was hidden in his shoe, which led to shoe removal at airport security checkpoints.

Fast forward to April 2003, pilots started to carry firearms and cockpits now have enhanced barriers to protect them from getting overthrown.

In 2008 canine units were implemented into the airport security forces. This allows for the dogs to sniff out bombs in bags and on passengers.
