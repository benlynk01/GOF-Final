Technological responses to terrorism

Technology in the current state

In June 2017 facial recognition and biometric screening were replacing boarding passes. This is a collaboration of Delta Air Lines and TSA. The system takes a photo of the passenger and matches it with the one on the airline's file.

This raised a significant privacy concern to many people though. People fear that the government would be able to track where a person is going anytime they board a flight.

TSA tried to expand this new and efficient technology, but the project was delayed due to Covid-19

In 2021 TSA has kept building their security. The agency has over 65,000 employees and spends billions of dollars in efforts to keep people safe.

TSA has also implemented air cargo screening, which checks 100% of luggage packed onto the plane. Before I only discussed carry-on items, such as backpacks, purses, and smaller bags.

TSA also uses millimeter wave advanced imaging technology to safely screen passengers for metallic and nonmetallic threats, including weapons and explosives.
